Trump's G7 Pivot Recasts the Ukraine War

特朗普在 G7 对乌克兰议题的态度转向

A Business English learning page based on scraped Reuters/Yahoo source notes about G7 diplomacy, Ukraine, Iran, and critical minerals.

来源 Reuters / Yahoo News 原文链接

At the G7 summit in Evian-les-Bains, Donald Trump joked that he was "the boss", a remark that captured how much the meeting still revolved around Washington's choices.

在埃维昂莱班举行的 G7 峰会上，特朗普开玩笑称自己是“老板”。这句话折射出一个现实：峰会很大程度上仍围绕华盛顿的选择展开。

The leaders issued a joint statement with a renewed pledge of support for Ukraine and backed fresh sanctions against Russia.

各国领导人发表联合声明，再次承诺支持乌克兰，并支持对俄罗斯实施新一轮制裁。

That unity may bolster Kyiv's leverage if future peace talks with Moscow begin.

如果未来与莫斯科展开和平谈判，这种团结可能会加强基辅的谈判筹码。

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskiy and European allies wanted Trump to see that Ukraine's battlefield position had improved and that Russia could not simply dictate terms.

乌克兰总统泽连斯基和欧洲盟友希望特朗普看到，乌克兰的战场处境已有改善，俄罗斯不能简单地强行规定和平条件。

Trump described Russia as the offensive party and said Moscow was losing more soldiers than Ukraine, which French President Emmanuel Macron called a real change in approach.

特朗普称俄罗斯是战争中的进攻方，并表示莫斯科损失的士兵多于乌克兰。法国总统马克龙认为这是美国态度的真实变化。

Still, any effort to strongarm Moscow into talks depends on US commitments, which can be politically elusive.

不过，任何试图强力迫使莫斯科谈判的行动，都取决于美国承诺，而这类承诺在政治上往往难以捉摸。

The summit also welcomed a preliminary deal between the United States and Iran, while diplomats warned that a robust agreement would be harder to secure.

峰会还欢迎美国与伊朗达成初步协议，但外交人士警告，要达成稳固协议会困难得多。

Trump stressed that the memorandum of understanding was not final, leaving room for another standoff if Tehran failed to comply.

特朗普强调，谅解备忘录并非最终协议；如果德黑兰未能履约，仍可能出现新的僵局。

Separately, G7 leaders agreed to coordinate on critical minerals, including stockpiling, to reduce dependence on China for metals used in defense, technology, and renewable energy.

另外，G7 领导人同意在关键矿产方面加强协调，包括战略储备，以减少国防、科技和可再生能源所需金属对中国的依赖。
