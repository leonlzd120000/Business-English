Trump Says Apple and Intel Will Work on U.S. Chips

特朗普称苹果与英特尔将在美国芯片上合作

A copyright-safe Business English lesson on Apple, Intel, U.S. chip manufacturing, and the politics of semiconductor supply chains.

来源 Yahoo Finance / Reuters 原文链接

President Donald Trump said Apple had agreed to work with Intel on chip design and manufacturing in the United States. For markets, the message was less about one product and more about the future of American semiconductor capacity.

美国总统唐纳德·特朗普表示，苹果已同意与英特尔在美国开展芯片设计和制造合作。对市场而言，这一信息关注的不只是某个产品，而是美国半导体产能的未来。

The possible partnership would give Apple another way to diversify its chip supply chain. The iPhone maker has depended heavily on TSMC, whose most advanced factories are also in high demand from AI chipmakers.

这一潜在合作将为苹果提供另一种分散芯片供应链的方式。苹果一直高度依赖台积电，而台积电最先进的产线也受到 AI 芯片厂商的强烈需求。

Investors reacted quickly. Intel shares rose in premarket trading, adding to a strong run this year, because an Apple contract would signal steadier demand for Intel's foundry business.

投资者迅速作出反应。英特尔股价在盘前上涨，并延续今年以来的强势表现，因为苹果订单将意味着英特尔代工业务拥有更稳定的需求。

Reuters noted that Apple and Intel did not immediately comment outside regular business hours. That caveat matters: political announcements can move markets before companies confirm the operational details.

路透社指出，苹果和英特尔在非工作时间没有立即回应置评请求。这个保留信息很重要：政治表态可能在企业确认具体运营细节之前就推动市场波动。

For Intel, a customer like Apple would be more than revenue. It would strengthen the company's reputation after years of trailing TSMC in advanced manufacturing.

对英特尔而言，苹果这样的客户带来的不只是收入。它还会增强公司的声誉，因为英特尔近年来在先进制程制造方面一直落后于台积电。

The timing also fits Intel's technology roadmap. The company recently said its 18A manufacturing process had entered initial production, a milestone it hopes will prove its factories can compete again.

这一时点也符合英特尔的技术路线图。该公司最近表示，其 18A 制造工艺已进入初始生产阶段，这是它希望证明其工厂能够重新竞争的重要里程碑。

The broader story is industrial policy. Washington has been trying to reduce reliance on overseas suppliers for critical minerals and semiconductors, including through direct investment and equity stakes.

更大的背景是产业政策。华盛顿一直试图减少对海外关键矿产和半导体供应商的依赖，其中包括直接投资和持股。

Such deals can blur the line between corporate strategy and government strategy. A chip factory is not only a business asset; it is also a national-security asset.

这类交易会模糊企业战略与政府战略之间的界限。芯片工厂不仅是商业资产，也是国家安全资产。

For business English learners, the key phrase is supply-chain resilience. Companies want cheaper production, but governments increasingly want production that is local, trusted, and politically secure.

对商务英语学习者来说，关键词是供应链韧性。企业希望生产成本更低，但政府越来越希望生产本地化、可信且具备政治安全性。

The final lesson is that chip news often combines technology, finance, and geopolitics. A single manufacturing agreement can affect stock prices, supplier relationships, and national policy at the same time.

最后的启示是，芯片新闻常常同时涉及技术、金融和地缘政治。一项制造协议可能同时影响股价、供应商关系和国家政策。
