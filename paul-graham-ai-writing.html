How AI Is Reshaping the Art of Writing

AI 如何重塑写作的艺术

A copyright-safe English learning package about what happens when language models take over the work of putting words on a page — and what that reveals about writing itself.

来源 Paul Graham 原文链接

Writing has always been a way of thinking. When you force an idea into sentences, you discover whether it is actually clear. That discipline—the friction of committing to words—is what AI threatens to remove.

写作始终是一种思考方式。当你把一个想法转化为句子时，你会发现它是否真正清晰。这种纪律——用语言表达的磨砺——正是AI所威胁要消除的东西。

Resumes were among the first documents to change. Hiring managers noticed something had shifted: every candidate now sounded polished, confident, and curiously identical. The authentic awkwardness that once revealed a person's genuine priorities had disappeared.

简历是最先发生变化的文件之一。招聘经理注意到一些变化：每位候选人现在听起来都很精练、自信，而且奇怪地千篇一律。那种曾经揭示一个人真实优先事项的真实笨拙感已经消失了。

Startup pitches showed the same pattern. Founders who once agonised over their opening lines were generating five versions in seconds. Investors grew wary. The tells were subtle—rounded statistics, generic enthusiasm, an absence of the specific failure that gives a founder credibility.

创业提案也呈现出同样的规律。曾经为开场白苦苦斟酌的创始人，如今几秒钟就能生成五个版本。投资者开始警惕。迹象很微妙——四舍五入的数据、泛泛的热情，以及缺少那种赋予创始人可信度的具体失败经历。

The word "delve" became an unofficial signature of AI-generated text. Language models reach for it constantly. A report that promised to delve into the data was almost certainly written by a machine, or at least heavily edited by one. Readers started noticing.

"delve"（深入探究）一词成为AI生成文本的非官方标志。语言模型不断使用它。一份承诺"深入研究数据"的报告几乎可以肯定是由机器撰写的，或至少经过机器大量修改。读者开始注意到这一点。

Experienced readers began developing what some called a slop-radar. It worked not by detecting individual words but by sensing the overall texture. AI text was smooth in the wrong way—like a surface with no grain, a voice with no history behind it.

有经验的读者开始发展出一种被称为"垃圾雷达"的能力。它不是通过检测单个词语来运作，而是感知整体质感。AI文本以一种错误的方式显得流畅——就像一个没有纹理的表面，一个背后没有历史的声音。

This reaction was a form of cognitive dissonance. The text was grammatically correct, even stylistically confident. Yet something felt wrong. Readers could not always name it, but they learned to trust the discomfort.

这种反应是一种认知失调。文本语法正确，甚至文体上也很自信。然而感觉有些不对劲。读者并不总能说出原因，但他们学会了相信这种不适感。

Hard-hitting journalism was particularly vulnerable to comparison. A well-reported story carries evidence of effort: the awkward quote that does not fit the narrative, the source who changed her mind, the fact that surprised even the reporter. AI text tends to be clean in a way that real reporting never is.

犀利的新闻报道在比较中尤其容易暴露差异。一篇经过深入报道的故事带有努力的痕迹：不符合叙事的别扭引语、改变了想法的消息来源、甚至让记者自己都感到意外的事实。AI文本往往以一种真实报道从不具备的方式显得干净整洁。

The phenomenon revealed a generational divide. AI-native users—those who had grown up with these tools—were less troubled by smooth text. They had been producing it since school. Writing, to them, was a task to complete rather than a skill to develop.

这一现象揭示了代际差异。AI原生用户——那些从小使用这些工具长大的人——对流畅的文本并不感到困扰。他们从上学起就在生产这类文本。对他们来说，写作是一项需要完成的任务，而不是需要培养的技能。

Older readers found this troubling in ways they struggled to articulate. The concern was not only about quality. It was about what writing reveals: how someone thinks, what they notice, whether they have a genuine point of view. AI could produce sentences; it could not produce a perspective earned through experience.

年长的读者发现这令人不安，但又难以言表。担忧不仅仅是关于质量。而是关于写作所揭示的东西：一个人如何思考、他们注意到什么、他们是否有真正的观点。AI可以生成句子，却无法产生通过经历获得的视角。

Some industry titans tried to reframe the debate. Writing tools, they argued, were no different from spell-checkers or calculators. The goal was to communicate; if AI helped, that was a feature, not a flaw. Critics thought this missed the point entirely.

一些行业巨头试图重新定义这场争论。他们认为，写作工具与拼写检查器或计算器并无不同。目标是沟通；如果AI有所帮助，那是一个特性，而非缺陷。批评者认为这完全没抓住要点。

There is an asymmetry that matters. A spell-checker fixes errors you made while thinking. AI writing replaces the thinking itself. The difference is significant in domains where the thinking is the product—law, medicine, strategy, any field where fluency can be mistaken for understanding.

有一种重要的不对称性。拼写检查器修正你在思考过程中犯的错误。AI写作替代了思考本身。这种差异在思考就是产品的领域中意义重大——法律、医学、战略，任何流利表达可能被误认为是理解的领域。

In those fields, AI writing became a kind of ruse. A legal brief that looked authoritative but was generated carelessly could contain confident errors. The reader's trust was exploited by the apparent fluency of the text. Collapse came only under direct questioning.

在这些领域，AI写作成了一种伎俩。一份看起来权威但草率生成的法律摘要可能包含自信的错误。读者的信任被文本表面的流利所利用。只有在直接质疑下，问题才会暴露。

Schools debated how to respond. Some banned AI writing tools outright. Others required students to show drafts at every stage. A few concluded that learning to prompt and edit AI was itself a valuable skill, and rebuilt their curricula around it. None of these answers felt entirely satisfying.

学校争论着如何应对。一些学校直接禁止AI写作工具。另一些要求学生在每个阶段都展示草稿。还有一些得出结论：学习如何提示和编辑AI本身就是一项有价值的技能，并围绕此重建了课程。这些答案都不令人完全满意。

The strongest argument for learning to write well remained simple: the discipline of writing is also the discipline of thinking clearly. Getting an idea onto a page reveals whether the idea is actually coherent. Outsourcing that process outsources the clarity check too.

学好写作最有力的理由仍然简单：写作的纪律也是清晰思考的纪律。将想法写在纸上，会揭示这个想法是否真正连贯。将这一过程外包，也就将清晰度检验外包了出去。

The market began to reflect this. Newsletters written in a recognisable human voice attracted loyal subscribers. Blogs that shared genuine uncertainty—about predictions, about methods, about failures—grew audiences steadily. Authenticity became a product differentiator.

市场开始反映这一点。以可辨识的人类声音撰写的时事通讯吸引了忠实的订阅者。那些坦诚分享真实不确定性——关于预测、方法、失败——的博客稳定地增长了受众。真实性成为了产品差异化的优势。

Paradoxically, the flood of AI-generated text may have made good human writing more valuable, not less. When average quality falls because anyone can produce fluent sentences, the writing that shows actual thought stands out more sharply than before.

矛盾的是，AI生成文本的泛滥可能使优质人类写作变得更有价值，而非更少。当任何人都能生成流利句子导致平均质量下降时，那些展现真实思考的写作比以往更加突出。

Companies with genuine writing cultures—where memos were expected to make clear arguments, and those arguments were expected to hold up—seemed to benefit. They had already been practicing the discipline that AI could not easily replicate: structured thinking made visible.

拥有真正写作文化的公司——备忘录被期望提出清晰论点，而这些论点被期望经得起推敲——似乎从中受益。他们已经在践行AI难以轻易复制的纪律：将结构化思维可视化。

The simplest test of whether writing was genuine remained unchanged: could the author answer questions about what they had written? AI-generated text often failed at this point. The words were there; the understanding that should have produced them sometimes was not.

检验写作是否真实的最简单测试保持不变：作者能否回答关于自己所写内容的问题？AI生成的文本往往在这一关失败。文字是有的；而本应产生这些文字的理解有时并不存在。

For learners of English, the lesson is practical. The words AI models overuse are worth knowing—partly to avoid them, and partly to understand why fluency alone is never enough. Real writing has friction. That friction is almost always the thinking. Remove it, and something essential disappears.

对于英语学习者来说，这一课是实用的。了解AI模型过度使用的词汇是有价值的——部分是为了避免使用它们，部分是为了理解为什么仅有流利是永远不够的。真正的写作有摩擦感。那种摩擦感几乎总是思考本身。去掉它，某种本质的东西就消失了。
